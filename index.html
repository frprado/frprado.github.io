Frederico Prado

MSc, Linguistics

Linguistics PhD student at McMaster University, Ontario, Canada. I have a BA in linguistics from Unicamp, São Paulo, Brazil. My main research area is theoretical syntax, my main interests being cross-clausal dependencies (specifically Hyper-Raising), empty categories and issues related to the A/A-bar distinction and discoursive operations. I am also interested in experimental syntax and the consequences the usage of formal experimental methods can bring in to fine tune our theoretical generalizations. Full CV

Other work

  • 2021 Experimental case study

    Assessing gramaticality judgement data on matrix null subjects in question enviroments in Brazilian Portuguese
    An experimental study of grammaticality judgements with a large sample of 400 Brazilian Portuguese speakers aiming to assess how these speakers judge pro-drop in the context of wh-movement in matrix clauses. Experimental design can be found on my github linked above. PDF


  • 2020 Manuscript

    Structural conditions for pro-drop in partial null subject languages, the case of matrix null subjects in Brazilian Portuguese
    A squib discussing the empirical and theoretical gaps in current approaches to Partial Null Subject Languages in light of data on pro-drop in Brazilian Portuguese and its apparent interaction with wh-movement. PDF
